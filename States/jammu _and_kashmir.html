The Mughal Gardens
Jammu and Kashmir
The Mughal Gardens in Jammu and Kashmir are a series of well-known gardens built by Mughal emperors in the 16th and 17th centuries. The most famous among them include Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi in Srinagar. These gardens are renowned for their stunning landscapes, terraced lawns, flowing water channels, and vibrant flowerbeds, all designed in the traditional Persian style. They often feature intricate fountains, cascading waterfalls, and pavilions, offering breathtaking views of the Dal Lake and the surrounding mountains. The Mughal Gardens are celebrated for their historical significance and exquisite beauty, making them popular attractions for tourists and nature lovers alike.
