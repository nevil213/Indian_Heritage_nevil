Konark Sun Temple
Odisha
The Konark Sun Temple, located in Odisha, India, is a 13th-century architectural marvel and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga dynasty, the temple is designed as a colossal chariot with intricately carved stone wheels, pillars, and walls, dedicated to the Sun God, Surya. Its intricate carvings depict scenes of daily life, mythology, and celestial beings. Although partly in ruins, the temple's grandeur and artistic excellence continue to attract visitors and scholars, symbolizing the zenith of Odisha's temple architecture and India's rich cultural heritage.

Lingaraj Temple
Odisha
Lingaraj Temple, located in Bhubaneswar, Odisha, is one of the oldest and largest temples in the state, dedicated to Lord Shiva. Built in the 11th century, it exemplifies the Kalinga architectural style with its towering spire, intricate carvings, and a massive courtyard housing numerous smaller shrines. The temple is known for its exquisite sculptures, depicting various Hindu deities, dancers, and mythological scenes. It is a significant pilgrimage site, attracting thousands of devotees and tourists. The temple's rich history and architectural grandeur make it an important symbol of India's cultural and religious heritage.
Place
Ratnagiri
Odisha
Ratnagiri, located in Odisha, India, is an ancient Buddhist site known for its significant archaeological and historical value. It was a prominent center of Mahayana Buddhism between the 5th and 13th centuries. The site features a large monastery complex with a well-preserved stupa, shrines, and numerous sculptures and carvings depicting Buddhist iconography. The ruins include exquisite depictions of Buddha and other deities, reflecting the artistic and cultural richness of the period. Ratnagiri is part of the "Diamond Triangle" of Odisha, along with Lalitgiri and Udayagiri, and serves as a key destination for understanding India's Buddhist heritage.
