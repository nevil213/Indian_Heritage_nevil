The architecture of the Bahmani and Deccan Sultanates, spanning from the 14th to the 17th centuries, is a remarkable fusion of Persian, Turkish, and indigenous Indian styles. Prominent structures include the Gol Gumbaz in Bijapur, with its massive dome and whispering gallery, and the Jama Masjid in Gulbarga, known for its grand arches and domes. The Bahmani Sultanate's capital, Bidar, features the impressive Bidar Fort and the ornate madrasa of Mahmud Gawan. These sultanates are also credited with creating unique Indo-Islamic garden tombs and mosques. The architectural legacy of the Bahmani and Deccan Sultanates reflects their rich cultural milieu and significant contributions to India's historical and architectural heritage.

galconda
telangana
Golconda Fort, located in Hyderabad, Telangana, India, is a monumental fortress renowned for its impressive military architecture and historical significance. Originally built in the 13th century by the Kakatiya dynasty and later expanded by the Qutb Shahi rulers, the fort features massive gates, robust bastions, and intricate water supply systems. It is famed for its acoustic marvels, such as the echoing clap at the entrance, and its grand Fateh Darwaza (Victory Gate). Golconda was once a flourishing center of trade and the diamond industry, home to the legendary Koh-i-Noor and Hope diamonds. This fort stands as a testament to India's rich cultural and architectural heritage.
