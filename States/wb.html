Shantiniketan
West Bengal
Shantiniketan, located in West Bengal, India, is a renowned cultural and educational center founded by Nobel laureate Rabindranath Tagore. Established in 1901 as an ashram-style school, it later evolved into Visva-Bharati University in 1921. Shantiniketan emphasizes a holistic education blending traditional Indian and Western philosophies, arts, and sciences. The campus is celebrated for its open-air classrooms, artistic heritage, and vibrant festivals. Tagore's vision of a place where the world meets in a spirit of mutual respect and learning continues to make Shantiniketan a symbol of cultural and intellectual heritage in India.
Place
Neora Valley National Park
West Bengal
Neora Valley National Park, located in West Bengal, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site celebrated for its rich biodiversity and pristine natural beauty. Established in 1986, the park covers approximately 88,000 hectares and is part of the Eastern Himalayas. It is home to a wide variety of flora and fauna, including rare species like the red panda and the clouded leopard. The park's diverse habitats, ranging from subtropical to alpine, support unique ecosystems and contribute to its ecological significance. Neora Valley is a vital conservation area and an important part of India's natural heritage.
