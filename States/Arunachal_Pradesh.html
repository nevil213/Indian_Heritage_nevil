Thembang Fortified Villagey
Arunachal Pradesh
The Thembang Fortified Village, located in Arunachal Pradesh, India, is a historic site known for its ancient stone architecture and strategic defensive features. This heritage village, part of the Monpa tribe, is perched on a hilltop offering panoramic views of the surrounding valley. The fort's walls and gates reflect traditional construction techniques and have stood the test of time. Thembang holds cultural and historical significance, showcasing the lifestyle and heritage of the Monpa community. It has been recognized for its unique cultural landscape and efforts to preserve its rich history.
Place
The Apatani Cultural Landscape
Arunachal Pradesh
The Apatani Cultural Landscape, located in Arunachal Pradesh, India, is renowned for its unique agricultural practices and sustainable living. The Apatani people are known for their intricate terrace farming and efficient irrigation systems, which have been maintained for centuries. The landscape also features traditional bamboo houses and the distinctive cultural practices of the Apatani tribe, including unique facial tattoos and nose plugs worn by older women. This cultural landscape exemplifies a harmonious relationship between humans and nature, preserving biodiversity while maintaining cultural heritage. The site has been recognized for its exceptional value in demonstrating sustainable land-use practices.
