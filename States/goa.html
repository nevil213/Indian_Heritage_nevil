Place
Sé Cathedral
Goa
Sé Cathedral, located in Old Goa, India, is one of the largest churches in Asia and a UNESCO World Heritage site. Dedicated to Saint Catherine, the cathedral was built to commemorate the Portuguese victory over the Muslim army, which led to the capture of Goa in 1510. Its construction began in 1562 and was completed in 1619, showcasing Portuguese-Manueline architectural style. The cathedral features a striking Corinthian interior, an impressive high altar, and a bell known as the "Golden Bell" for its rich tone. Sé Cathedral stands as a testament to Goa's colonial history and its significance in the spread of Christianity in Asia.
Place
Basilica of Bom Jesus
Goa
The Basilica of Bom Jesus, located in Old Goa, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site renowned for its baroque architecture. Built between 1594 and 1605, the basilica houses the mortal remains of St. Francis Xavier, a revered Christian missionary. The church's name means "Holy Jesus" and it signifies the reverence of the infant Jesus. Its facade is a blend of Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian styles, while the interior features elaborate altars and intricately carved wooden panels. The Basilica of Bom Jesus is a significant pilgrimage site and a symbol of Goa's colonial past and its role in the spread of Christianity in India.
Place
Fort Aguada
Goa
Fort Aguada, located in Goa, India, is a well-preserved 17th-century Portuguese fort overlooking the Arabian Sea. Built in 1612, it served as a crucial defense point against Dutch and Maratha invasions, protecting the Portuguese territory. The fort's name, "Aguada," comes from the Portuguese word for water, as it housed a freshwater spring that supplied water to passing ships. The fort complex includes a four-story lighthouse, one of the oldest of its kind in Asia, and a large cistern that could store over two million gallons of water. Fort Aguada is a significant cultural and historical landmark, representing Goa's colonial past and architectural heritage. Its scenic location and robust structure make it a popular tourist destination.
