Great himalayan national park
Himachal Pradesh
Great Himalayan National Park, located in Himachal Pradesh, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage site celebrated for its rich biodiversity and stunning landscapes. Established in 1984, the park covers over 1,171 square kilometers and features a diverse range of ecosystems, from subtropical forests to alpine meadows. It is home to a variety of rare and endangered species, including the snow leopard, the western tragopan, and the Himalayan tahr. The park's rugged terrain and pristine environment provide crucial habitat for these species and offer exceptional opportunities for scientific research and eco-tourism. Its preservation of high-altitude ecological zones makes it a vital heritage site for conservation.
Place
McLeod Ganj
Himachal Pradesh
McLeod Ganj, located in Himachal Pradesh, India, is renowned as the residence of the Dalai Lama and a vibrant center of Tibetan culture. Nestled in the Himalayas, this small town is a hub for Tibetan Buddhism and attracts visitors from around the world for its monasteries, spiritual teachings, and cultural festivals. Established during British rule in the 19th century, McLeod Ganj has become a focal point for Tibetan exile culture and heritage, featuring traditional Tibetan art, cuisine, and crafts. The town's serene setting and cultural significance make it a unique and important destination for both spiritual seekers and heritage enthusiasts.
