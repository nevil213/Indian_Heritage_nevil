Padmanabhapuram Palace
Kerala
Padmanabhapuram Palace, located in the town of Padmanabhapuram in Tamil Nadu, India, is a magnificent example of traditional Kerala architecture. Constructed in the 16th century, it was the seat of the Travancore kingdom's rulers. The palace is renowned for its intricate wooden carvings, murals, and a unique blend of Kerala and Dravidian architectural styles. Key features include the King's Council Chamber (Mantrasala), the Queen's Palace (Uppirikka Malika), and a clock tower over 200 years old. The palace's historical and architectural significance makes it an important heritage site in India.
