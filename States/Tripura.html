Angkor Wat of the Northeast
Tripura
The term "Angkor Wat of the Northeast" often refers to the temple complex of Sankaram (or Sankaram) in the Northeast Indian state of Tripura. This site, known for its impressive and intricate carvings, bears resemblance to the architectural style of Angkor Wat in Cambodia. The temples are part of the ancient Hindu heritage of the region and showcase significant artistic and architectural achievements. Sankaram is recognized for its historical and cultural importance, drawing visitors interested in exploring the rich heritage of Northeast India.
