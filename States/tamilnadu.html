Place
Brihadeshwar Temple
Tamilnadu
Brihadeshwara Temple, also known as Rajarajeswaram or Peruvudaiyar Kovil, is located in Thanjavur, Tamil Nadu, India, and is a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Built by Chola emperor Rajaraja I between 1003 and 1010 CE, this magnificent temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is an exemplary piece of Dravidian architecture. The temple is renowned for its grand vimana (tower) that rises over 200 feet, intricate sculptures, and stunning frescoes. The colossal Nandi statue carved out of a single stone further highlights the temple's architectural marvels. Brihadeshwara Temple stands as a testament to the Chola dynasty's architectural ingenuity and India's rich cultural and religious heritage.
Place
Great Living Chola
Tamilnadu
The Great Living Chola Temples, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, are a trio of monumental temples in Tamil Nadu, India, built during the Chola dynasty (9th to 13th centuries). This group includes the Brihadeshwara Temple at Thanjavur, the Brihadeshwara Temple at Gangaikonda Cholapuram, and the Airavatesvara Temple at Darasuram. These temples are celebrated for their grand scale, architectural precision, and intricate sculptures depicting various deities and mythological scenes. They reflect the zenith of Dravidian architecture and the Cholas' contribution to Tamil culture, art, and religion. The temples stand as a testament to the dynasty's engineering prowess and its enduring legacy in India's cultural heritage.
Place
Mahabalipuram
Telangana
Mahabalipuram, also known as Mamallapuram, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Tamil Nadu, India, renowned for its group of monuments and rock-cut architecture. Dating back to the 7th and 8th centuries during the Pallava dynasty, the site includes intricately carved temples, cave sanctuaries, and giant open-air rock reliefs, such as the famous "Descent of the Ganges." The Shore Temple, a masterpiece of Dravidian architecture, stands prominently by the Bay of Bengal. These monuments highlight the artistic and architectural prowess of the Pallavas and reflect the rich cultural and religious history of ancient South India. Mahabalipuram is a testament to India's enduring legacy of artistic excellence.
Place
Nilgiri Railway
Telangana
The Mountain Railways of India, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, comprise three historic railways: the Darjeeling Himalayan Railway, the Nilgiri Mountain Railway, and the Kalka-Shimla Railway. These railways, built between the late 19th and early 20th centuries, are engineering marvels that traverse through the rugged and scenic landscapes of the Himalayas and Western Ghats. They showcase exceptional engineering skills, including steep gradients and sharp curves, and utilize unique technologies such as the rack and pinion system in the Nilgiri Railway. The Mountain Railways of India are celebrated not only for their technical brilliance but also for their significant role in the socio-economic development and tourism of their respective regions. They stand as a testament to India's rich industrial heritage and scenic beauty.
