Keibul Lamjao National Park
Manipur
Keibul Lamjao National Park, located in Manipur, India, is the only floating national park in the world. It is renowned for its unique phumdis, floating biomass formations that create a habitat for diverse wildlife. The park is home to the endangered Sangai deer, Manipur's state animal, and supports a variety of flora and fauna. Spread across Loktak Lake, Keibul Lamjao is a crucial area for biodiversity and conservation. Its distinctive ecosystem and efforts to protect the Sangai deer make it a significant environmental and tourist destination.
