Place
Hampi
Karnataka
Hampi, located in Karnataka, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and the former capital of the Vijayanagara Empire. Flourishing in the 14th to 16th centuries, Hampi was one of the largest and wealthiest cities of its time. The site is renowned for its stunning Dravidian architecture, including temples, palaces, and market streets. Key structures include the Virupaksha Temple, Vittala Temple with its iconic stone chariot, and the Lotus Mahal. Hampi's ruins, set amidst a surreal landscape of rocky hills and boulders, provide a vivid glimpse into the rich cultural and architectural achievements of medieval South India.
Place
Pattadakal
Karnataka
Pattadakal, situated in Karnataka, India, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site renowned for its exquisite 7th and 8th-century temples that showcase a harmonious blend of Dravidian and Nagara architectural styles. This historic site served as a royal center for the Chalukya dynasty, where kings were crowned and celebrated. The temple complex includes notable structures like the Virupaksha Temple and the Mallikarjuna Temple, both rich in intricate carvings and sculptures depicting various Hindu deities and legends. The artistic and architectural achievements of Pattadakal reflect the cultural and religious diversity of early medieval India, making it a significant heritage site.
Place
Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas
Karnataka
The Sacred Ensembles of the Hoysalas, located in Karnataka, India, are a UNESCO World Heritage Site comprising the temples of Belur, Halebidu, and Somnathapura. These temples, built during the 12th and 13th centuries under the Hoysala dynasty, are renowned for their intricate stone carvings, detailed sculptures, and distinctive star-shaped platforms. Notable temples include the Chennakeshava Temple in Belur and the Hoysaleswara Temple in Halebidu, both of which exhibit exquisite craftsmanship depicting deities, dancers, and scenes from Hindu epics. The Hoysala temples are celebrated for their unique architectural style and are significant examples of Indian temple art and architecture.
Place
Bidar fort
Karnataka
Bidar Fort, located in Karnataka, India, is a significant historical monument known for its impressive architecture and rich history. Built in the 15th century by Sultan Ala-ud-Din Bahman of the Bahmani Sultanate, the fort features a unique blend of Persian, Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. The complex includes grand gateways, palaces, mosques, gardens, and intricate tile work, with the Rangin Mahal being a notable highlight for its ornate decoration. Bidar Fort served as a major political and cultural center during the Bahmani and Barid Shahi dynasties. It remains an important heritage site, reflecting the medieval Deccan's architectural and cultural heritage.
